I am a theoretical physicist with experience in modeling chemical and biological systems and bioinformatics. My research interests lie at the intersection of biophysics and biomolecular evolution, exploring the complex mechanisms and evolutionary processes that govern biological systems.

Currently, I am a Research Scientist at the Blue Marble Space Institute of Science (BMSIS) and a visiting researcher at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of California, Santa Barbara (UCSB) and a Graduate Research Fellow at the Spanish Center of Astrobiology (CAB, CSIC-INTA).

I consider myself a collaborative researcher with a strong interest in astrobiology. My research is dedicated to seeking a better understanding of the physicochemical and evolutionary processes that may have been present in early forms of life. I focus this understanding with a theoretical point-of-view, employing computational tools for calculations and simulations to support my results. I am also interested in interdisciplinary research with biomedical applications.
